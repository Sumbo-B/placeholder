5.ANNOUNCEMENT
The Pastor shall say:
Dearly beloved, we are gathered here before God and this Congregation, to join together this man and this woman in Holy Matrimony which is an honourable estate established by God before the fall of Man, signifying to us the mystical union between Christ and His Church.
This Holy estates Christ both honored and beautified with his presence at the wedding in Cana of Galilee where He performed His first miracle. It is also commended by Saint Paul to be of great honour among all men. It is therefore not a thing to be rushed into, or undertaken or instituted lightly to satisfy your carnal lust appetites, like brute beast that have no understanding, but reverently, discreetly, advisedly, soberly and in the fear of GOD, carefully considering the cause for which matrimony was ordained.
FIRSTLY:
It was ordained for mutual Companionship, help and comfort that one ought to have of the other both in prosperity and adversity.
SECONDLY:
It was ordained for a remedy against sin and to avoid fornication that such person who have not the gift of continency might marry and keep themselves undefiled members of Christ's Body.
THIRDLY:
It was ordained for procreation of Children to be brought in the fear of and nature of the Lord of the praise of His Holy Name.
Into this Holy estate these two persons here present have now come be joined. Therefore, if any man can show any just cause why they may not be lawfully joined together let him now speak or else hereafter forever hold his peace.

5. IFILO
Nigba naa ni Olusoaguntan yo wipe:
Olufe owon, awa pejo niwaju Oluwa ati ni Oju ijo eniyan Re lati da Okunrin yi ati Obinrin yi po ni igbeyawo mimo ti ise ipo ti o ni ola ti Olorun ti da sile saaju isubu Eniyan. O si je akawe isokan ti o wa laarin Kristi oun ijo Re.
Ipo mimo yii ni Kristi se loso ti o si se lewa nigbati O lo si ibi Igbeyawo ni Kana ti Galili, ti o si se ise iyanu akose Re nibe. Eyi na ni Poolu si yin pe o ni Ola laarin gbogbo eniyan. Nitorinaa, ki se ohun ti a ba ma kugbu se, ko ye ki a fi owo lee ni airotele bi ohun ti ko wuwo tabi ohun afire se, lati ma te ifekufe ara eniyan lorun, bi eranko ti ko ni iye ninu. Sugbon towotowo, toyetoye, tirotiro, ni pele ati ni iberu Olorun, ki a maa roo bi o ti ye. Nitori idi kini a se da igbeyawo sile?
IDI KINNI:
A daa sile fun ajumo kegbe, iranlowo ati itunu ti o ye ki eni kinni ki o ni lodo enikeji, ni irora ati ni iponju.
IDI KEJI
A daa sile ki o le se idena si ese ati ki a le ye ona agbere sile. Nitori wipe iru awon eniti ko ni ebun imaraduro, ki won ki o gbeyawo ki won ki o si pa ara won mo ni eya ara Kristi ti ko ni eeri.
IDI KETA:
A daa sile nitori Omo bibi ti a o le to ni iberu ati ni eko ti Olorun ati si iyin Oruko Mimo Re.

Ipo mimo yi ni awon eniyan mejeeji yi wa si lati da won po sinu re, nitorina bi enikan bari ohun ikose tooto kan, nitori eyi ti a ki yio fi da won po bi o ti to si nipa ofin ati ilana Olorun ki o wi ni iwoyi bi ko ba si se bee, lati oni lo ki o pa enu re mo titi lae.

6. DECLARATION
Pastor's question to the person to be married
(The Couple remain standing) the Bride shall be unveiled by the groom.
I require and charge you both, as you will answer, the secret of all hearts shall be disclosed that if either of you knows any impediment, why you may not be lawfully joined together in matrimony, you now have to confess it. For be it known to you that so many as are joined together against the laid down principles in the word of God are not joined together by God and their matrimony is not lawful.
THE DECLARATION OF THE MAN
If there is no impediment; the Pastor shall say to the man USMAN OLASUMBO BELLO will you have this woman OLUWASEYI PELUMI OLAONIPEKUN as your wedded wife, to live together according to God's ordinance in the Holy estate of Matrimony? Will you love Her, comfort Her, honour and keep Her in sickness and in health and forsaking all others, keep yourself only unto Her, so long as you both shall live?
The man shall answer: I will by God's Grace
THE DECLARATION OF THE WOMAN
Then the Pastor shall say to the woman
OLUWASEYI PELUMI OLAONIPEKUN will you have this man USMAN OLASUMBO BELLO as your wedded husband, to live together according to God's ordinance in the Holy estate of matrimony? Will you obey Him, serve Him and forsaking all others, keep yourself holy unto him so long as both shall live?
The woman shall answer: I will by God's Grace
Then shall the Pastor say: Who gives this woman to be married to this man?
(The father or one of the relations of the woman shall take her by the right hand and hand over to the Pastor)
6. IBEERE ATIIDAHUN PELU IJEWO
(Ni idide duro Oko Ataya)
Oluso aguntan yoo bere lowo awon ti a fe dapo ni igbeyawo mimo.
Mo beere lowo eyin mejeeji, bi o ti se pe eyin ni yio dahun ni ojo idajo ti o ni eru pupo, nigbati ohun ikoko okan gbogbo eniyan yoo si sile, bi enikan ninu eyin mejeeji ba mo ohun ikose kan, ti ki yoo je ki a da yin po ni igbeyawo bi o ti ye si nipa ofin, ki o ti iwoyi jewo re, nitori ki o da yin loju pe iye awon ti a ba dapo lodi si bi oro Olorun ti la sile fun ni, Olorun ko da won po bee ni igbeyawo won kii se nipa ofin.
IJEWO OKUNRIN
Biko basi ohun ikose; Olusoaguntan yio wi fun Okunrin naa pe USMAN OLASUMBO BELLO se iwo fe gbe obinrin yi OLUWASEYI PELUMI OLAONIPEKUN ni iyawo, ki eyin ki o jumo maa gbe po gege bi idasile Olorun ni ipo mimo igbeyawo? Se iwo o maa fee, iwo o maa tuu ninu? Iwo o maa bu ola fun Iwo yio ma se itoju re ni igba aisan ati ni igba ilera ni? Se iwo gba lati ko gbogbo awon elomiran sile ki iwo ki o si fi ara mo oun nikan soso titi iwon igbati eyin mejeeji yoo wa ni aye?
Okunrin naa yoo dahun pe: Beeni, mo fe, ki Olorun ran mi lowo.
IJEWO OBINRIN
Omidan OLUWASEYI OLAONIPEKUN PELUMI, se iwo fe ni Okunrin yi USMAN OLASUMBO BELLO ni oko re, ki eyin ki o jumo maa gbe po gege bi idasile Olorun ni ipo mimo igbeyawo? Se iwo o ma gbo tire? Iwo o ma sin in? Iwo o ma fee? Iwo o maa bu ola fun un? Iwo o ma se itoju re nigba aisan ati nigba ilera? Se iwo gba lati ko gbogbo awon elomiran sile ki iwo ki o si fi ara mo oun nikan soso titi iwon igbati eyin mejeeji yoo wa ni aye?
Obinrin naa yoo dahun pe: Beeni, mo fe, ki Olorun ran mi lowo.
Nigbana ni Olusoaguntan yio wipe: Tani o fi Obinrin yi fun Okunrin yi lati gbe ni iyawo?
(Baba tabi okan ninu ebi ti won ba yan, yio fa owo otun obinrin naa le Olusoaguntan na lowo)

7. The Vows
(The Pastor shall cause the groom to hold the bride with his right hand and say after the Pastor as follows:)
I USMAN OLASUMBO BELLO take you, OLUWASEYI PELUMI OLAONIPEKUN to be my wedded wife to have and to hold, from this day forward, for the better, for worse, for richer for poorer, in sickness and in health, to love, to cherish and to obey, till death do us part, according to God's ordinance and there to, I pledge you my truth.
(Then shall the Woman, holding the man's right in her own right hand, say after the Pastor as follows)
I OLUWASEYI PELUMI OLAONIPEKUN take you, USMAN OLASUMBO BELLO to be my wedded husband to have and to hold from this day forward, for poorer, in sickness and in health; to love, to cherish and obey, till death do us part according to God's Holy ordinance and to there to, I pledge you my truth.
8. Receiving the Holy Bible
Then shall the two of them hold a New Holy Bible presented by the Man. The Man will say after the Pastor as follows:
With this Holy Bible which is the word of God I wed you, with my body, I honour you and all my worldly goods I endow you; in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit.
Amen
9. Prayer for the Couple
Then the married couple shall kneel down and the Pastor shall pray for them.
10. Warning
Then the Pastor shall join the couple's right hand together and say:
Those who God has joined, together let no man put asunder. For as much as USMAN OLASUMBO BELLO and OLUWASEYI PELUMI OLAONIPEKUN have agreed to live together in Holy Wedlock and have witnessed the same before God and his congregration and thereto have vowed to each other and have declared the same by giving and receiving the Holy Bible, which is the word of God and everlasting witness; and by joining of hands, I pronounce that they be living together as HUSBAND and WIFE from today. In the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Ghost.
Amen.
